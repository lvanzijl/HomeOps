This document defines the canonical FamilyBoard marketing household for VisualReview marketing fixtures, marketing videos, product screenshots, documentation, website imagery, and future demonstrations.

## Household Story

### Weekday Routine

Weekdays start around 06:45. Mark makes coffee and checks the FamilyBoard home screen while Eva gets breakfast started. Thomas needs reminders for shoes, school bag, and football days. Robin wants to choose a cup, then usually needs help getting dressed. School drop-off is at 08:20 and daycare drop-off follows at 08:35.

Afternoons are staggered. Eva usually handles school pickup on Tuesdays and Thursdays. Mark handles football practice on Wednesday and Saturday. Dinner is simple on activity days: pasta, wraps, soup, or leftovers. FamilyBoard is checked again after dinner for tomorrow's agenda and tasks.

### Weekend Routine

Saturday is for football, errands, playground time, and visiting family when energy allows. Sunday is calmer: laundry, meal planning, library books, and a short Weekly Reset after Robin is in bed. Thomas joins the first part of the reset to celebrate helpful moments and choose one small focus for the coming week.

### Family Values

- Everyone helps in a way that fits their age.
- Routines should reduce stress, not create a perfect household.
- Appreciation is more important than points or rewards.
- Planning is shared visibly so one parent is not the only memory keeper.
- The children should see the family working as a team.

### How FamilyBoard Fits Naturally

FamilyBoard is a visible family dashboard in the kitchen/living area. In the morning it answers: “What matters today?” In the evening it answers: “What do we need for tomorrow?” On Sunday it helps the family close the week gently: keep what worked, carry forward what still matters, and notice the small helpful moments.

## Consistency Rules for Future Marketing Fixtures

1. This is the canonical FamilyBoard marketing household.
2. Future marketing fixtures must derive household data from this document instead of inventing new names, ages, roles, avatars, or routines.
3. Mark is always Dad, age 38; Eva is always Mom, age 36; Thomas is always age 6; Robin is always age 2.
4. Thomas is always the school-age child; Robin is always the toddler in daycare.
5. Avatar concepts must remain visually stable unless a later design change explicitly updates this document.
6. Tasks must align with agenda events. For example, swimming tasks only appear near swimming lessons.
7. Shopping must align with meals, children, household needs, and upcoming events.
8. Appreciations must reference real completed tasks, realistic child behavior, or agenda-supported moments.
9. Motivation must remain encouragement-first. Do not turn helpful moments into money, points economy, store purchases, or sibling competition.
10. Weekly Reset must reflect the same week shown in Home, Agenda, Tasks, Shopping, and Motivation.
11. Home should show a compact summary of the same underlying story, not unrelated demo content.
12. The calendar may be busy, but fixtures should preserve breathable family realism and avoid every day feeling overloaded.
13. Dutch supermarkets should remain realistic: Albert Heijn, Jumbo, Kruidvat, HEMA, and a local baker are acceptable.
14. Settings content should stay family-facing and avoid technical implementation details.
15. Production code, APIs, database schema, fixtures, tests, screenshots, and binaries must not be changed to update this design document.
