# HomeOps Product Experience Review — Round 4 — Child Persona Age 10

## Reviewer lens
Age 10 child. Wants ownership, progress, and achievement. This review evaluates current product experience only.

## Section 1 — Home

### Strengths
- I can see where my family’s things are: agenda, tasks, lists, and motivation.
- I can open my own page from the family strip.
- I can see the family goal if I look at the Motivation card.

### Weaknesses
- Home feels mostly for parents.
- I do not immediately see what I personally should do or what I helped with.
- The family goal is visible but not the main thing.

### Score
Good

### Reviewer verdict
Home is useful, but it feels like a family dashboard for adults more than a place for me.

## Section 2 — Child Workspace

### Mandatory questions

#### What does a child see first?
I see my avatar, my name, my goal, and progress.

#### What does a parent see first?
A parent sees that there is Child Mode and Parent Mode.

#### Does the page belong to the child or the parent?
It mostly belongs to me in Child Mode, but some wording still feels managed by parents.

### Strengths
- I like seeing my own progress.
- I like seeing how my effort contributes to the family goal.
- The page does not compare me with siblings.
- Helpful Moments make me feel recognized.
- Celebration gives the progress a reason.
- Parent Mode being separate makes the main page feel less like a settings page.

### Weaknesses
- I want more ownership: choosing a focus, saying what I am proud of, or seeing my contribution history in a positive way.
- Some sections tell me what adults assigned rather than letting me feel in charge.
- Celebration could be more motivating if it felt like something I helped plan.
- The page has progress, but not a strong sense of achievement identity.

### Score
Good

### Reviewer verdict
This feels like a child experience, not just a management screen. For my age, it needs more ownership and achievement to be really engaging.

## Section 3 — Motivation

### Strengths
- I understand the family goal and personal goals.
- Progress is clear.
- Stars/checkmarks make personal goals easy to understand.
- I like that it is not a competition.

### Weaknesses
- It still feels like parents create and edit everything.
- I want to feel more like I own my goal.
- The celebration is not exciting enough by itself.

### Score
Good

### Reviewer verdict
Motivation feels partly motivating and partly administrative. I understand why I should care, but I need more personal ownership.

## Section 4 — Helpful Moments

### Strengths
- “We noticed” feels good.
- Thank-you notes make effort visible.
- Tags help show what kind of effort mattered.
- It feels better than just checking off chores.

### Weaknesses
- It could feel like a feed unless special moments stand out.
- I want to see the best moments or things I am proud of.
- It would be stronger if Helpful Moments helped tell the family story more clearly.

### Score
Good

### Reviewer verdict
Helpful Moments feel like things my family appreciates, not just activity history.

## Section 5 — Celebration

### Mandatory question: Why would a child look forward to this?
I would look forward to Celebration if it is something I helped choose and if I can see that my progress is helping us get there. Right now I can see it, but I do not fully feel like I own it.

### Strengths
- Celebration gives a reason for family progress.
- Ready-to-celebrate is clear.
- Memory makes completed goals feel like they mattered.

### Weaknesses
- It needs more anticipation.
- It should show why the celebration is special to us, not just that it exists.
- “Mark celebrated” is an adult action.
- I want to feel like I helped unlock it or choose part of it.

### Score
Needs Work

### Reviewer verdict
Celebration is a good idea but not important enough yet. It should feel like the payoff for our family effort.

## Section 6 — Family Story

### Strengths
- Goal to progress to celebration to memory makes sense.
- I can see that my efforts are part of the bigger family goal.
- Memories make past achievements visible.

### Weaknesses
- The story feels spread out.
- Helpful Moments, tasks, and goals do not always feel like one connected journey.
- I need stronger “I helped make this happen” feedback.

### Score
Good

### Reviewer verdict
It is almost one family story, but it still feels like connected sections.

## Section 7 — Age Differentiation

### Strengths
- I get more progress detail than a younger child.
- The page is understandable for me.

### Weaknesses
- I want more control and responsibility.
- Progress detail alone is not enough to make it age-appropriate.
- It should feel less like a parent-managed checklist and more like my own place.

### Score
Needs Work

### Reviewer verdict
There is some meaningful difference, but for older kids ownership needs to be stronger.

## Section 8 — Product Cohesion

### Strengths
- My page, Motivation, Tasks, and Home are connected.
- The family goal appears in more than one place.
- The product has a consistent family tone in the motivation areas.

### Weaknesses
- Lists do not feel connected to me unless I have a role.
- Tasks can feel separate from appreciation and celebration.
- Some areas feel like parent tools while others feel like family encouragement.

### Score
Good

### Reviewer verdict
It mostly feels like one product, but not every part feels equally connected to the family story.

## Section 9 — Biggest missing piece

### One major improvement
Give kids more ownership in the family goal and celebration.

### Why
At age 10, I want to feel that I am helping shape the goal, not just being tracked by it.

### Product problem solved
It would make Motivation feel less parent-managed and more personally meaningful.

### Experience change
I would care more about progress because I helped choose what we are working toward or how we celebrate.
