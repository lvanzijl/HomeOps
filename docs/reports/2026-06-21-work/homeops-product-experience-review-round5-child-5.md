# HomeOps Product Experience Review Round 5 — Child Persona Age 5

## Reviewer Lens
Fun, recognition, simplicity.

## Independent Verdict
I can tell this page is about me and my family. I like seeing me, stars, thank-yous, and the fun thing we are going to do. I still want it to look more fun when the celebration is ready.

## Section 1 — Celebration Assessment

### Planned
I like knowing the fun family thing is coming. If it says we are getting closer, I understand that helping matters.

### ReadyToCelebrate
ReadyToCelebrate is exciting if it says “we did it.” I would want it to look big and happy.

### Celebrated
Celebrated is nice because it says we really did the fun thing.

### Memory
Memories are good if I can remember the fun thing. I care more if it looks special and happy.

### Core Question
Celebration feels like **A) the thing we are working toward** when I can see the fun thing and how close we are. Sometimes it still feels like grown-ups writing down what happened.

### Strengths
- I can see the celebration.
- I understand “we did it.”
- I like that my help gets us closer.
- Memories help me remember happy family things.

### Weaknesses
- It needs to look more exciting.
- Too many words are hard.
- I want to feel like the celebration is for all of us and I helped.

### Reviewer Verdict
Celebration is good, but make it more fun.

**Score: Good**

## Section 2 — Family Story Assessment
Goal → Progress → Celebration → Memory mostly makes sense if someone says it simply: help today, get closer, celebrate, remember.

### Strong Transitions
- Progress → Celebration: I can understand closer.
- Celebration → Memory: I can remember fun things we did.

### Weak Transitions
- “Goal” is a grown-up word.
- I may not understand why all the cards are connected unless they say it simply.

### Missing Emotional Links
Tell me: “Your help made movie night closer.”

### Mandatory Question
Could I explain “Why do goals exist?” I might say: “So we can help and get the fun family thing.” That is enough.

### Reviewer Verdict
Mostly one story if the words stay small.

**Score: Good**

## Section 3 — Child Experience Assessment

### What I Notice First
I notice me: my picture, my name, stars, and what I should do.

### What Excites Me
- Stars/checks.
- Thank-you notes.
- The family celebration.
- Seeing that I helped.

### What I Would Ignore
Parent buttons and lots of words.

### Does the Page Belong to Me?
It feels like **A) My Place In The Family** because it shows me and how I help.

### Strengths
- I can see myself.
- I know what to do today.
- My family says thank you.
- The celebration sounds fun.

### Weaknesses
- Some words are too grown-up.
- It could be more colorful and exciting.
- I need a parent if there is too much text.

### Reviewer Verdict
I like it. It feels like my family sees me.

**Score: Good**

## Section 4 — Helpful Moments Assessment
Helpful Moments feel like **A) Things My Family Appreciates**.

### Strengths
- I like “thank you.”
- I like “we noticed.”
- It makes me feel proud.
- It makes me want to help again.

### Weaknesses
- I may not read long notes.
- I want them to look special.

### Reviewer Verdict
This is my favorite part.

**Score: Excellent**

## Section 5 — Home Assessment
Home feels like a family place if it shows what we are doing today and the celebration. I would not use everything there by myself.

### Strengths
- Shows family things.
- Shows the celebration.
- Helps parents know today.

### Weaknesses
- It has grown-up stuff.
- It may look busy.

### Reviewer Verdict
More **A) Family Hub** for my parents; I like my own child page more.

**Score: Good**

## Section 6 — Motivation Assessment
Motivation feels like helping the family when it says the celebration is closer.

### Strengths
- I can understand closer.
- I like seeing progress.
- Celebration gives me a reason.

### Weaknesses
- Goal words can be hard.
- I care more about my page than a grown-up motivation page.

### Mandatory Question
Would I understand why progress matters? Yes, if it says my help gets us closer to the fun family thing.

### Reviewer Verdict
Good when simple.

**Score: Good**

## Section 7 — Age Differentiation Assessment

### Ages 3–5
This is for me when it has pictures, stars, checks, and short words.

### Ages 6–12
Bigger kids can read more and understand goals better.

### Strengths
- Younger kids get simpler things.
- Older kids get more details.

### Weaknesses
- Younger kids need even less text.
- Bigger kids might want more control.

### Reviewer Verdict
Good.

**Score: Good**

## Section 8 — Product Cohesion Assessment
It feels like one product when it says: help today, family gets closer, celebrate, remember.

### Strong Cohesion
- My page, thank-yous, and celebration fit together.
- Home shows family things.

### Weak Cohesion
- Lists are not exciting to me.
- Some pages feel for grown-ups.

### Reviewer Verdict
Mostly one family product.

**Score: Good**

## Section 9 — Biggest Remaining Problem
The biggest problem is that Celebration should feel bigger and more fun.

### Largest Experience Gain
Make the ready celebration moment look and feel like a happy family party moment.
