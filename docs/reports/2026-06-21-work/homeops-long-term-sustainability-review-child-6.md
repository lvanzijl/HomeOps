# HomeOps Long-Term Sustainability Review — Child Persona Age 6

## Independent Verdict
HomeOps **mostly** saves effort for my family because it shows me what to do and lets me feel noticed. I would keep liking it if it stays simple and does not have too many old things on the screen.

## Section 1 — Daily Friction Assessment
- **Actions required:** Look at my tasks, do one, mark it done, see something happy.
- **Maintenance required:** I do not want to clean up the app. A grown-up should make it stay tidy.
- **Data entry required:** I do not want to type much.
- **Repetitive:** Doing the same chores every day can get boring.
- **Unnecessary-feeling:** Reading too many words or seeing tasks that are not for today.

**Determine:** A) **Reduce effort** for me because it tells me what to do.

### Strengths
- I can understand simple tasks.
- I like being noticed.
- A short routine can help me know what comes next.

### Weaknesses
- Too many old tasks would confuse me.
- If celebration feels the same every time, I may stop caring.

**Reviewer Verdict:** Good if it stays clean and simple.  
**Score:** Good

## Section 2 — Recurring Task Assessment
Daily chores are okay if I can see today’s job. I do not want yesterday’s jobs stacking up.

- **Daily chores:** Good when there are only a few.
- **Weekly chores:** Okay if a parent explains them.
- **Monthly chores:** Hard to remember.

### Long-Term Strengths
- Helps me know my routine.
- Makes helping feel normal.

### Long-Term Weaknesses
- Same tasks can become boring.
- Old missed chores would feel unfair.

**Reviewer Verdict:** Good because missed chores should not pile up.  
**Score:** Good

## Section 3 — Goal Hygiene Assessment
One family goal and one goal for me is enough. More goals would be too many.

- **Keep active:** Yes, if I can understand it.
- **Ignore:** If it is too grown-up.
- **Archive:** A grown-up can hide old goals.
- **Abandon:** If it stops feeling fun or important.

**Reviewer Verdict:** One goal is simple.  
**Score:** Good

## Section 4 — Shopping Assessment
Shopping is mostly for grown-ups. I might help add something if it is easy.

- **Weekly shopping:** Good for parents.
- **Multi-store usage:** I do not care.
- **Trustworthiness:** I trust it if my parent trusts it.

### What Becomes Annoying After Months
- If I have to read long lists.
- If old crossed-out things stay around too long.

**Reviewer Verdict:** Good for the family, not my main reason to use HomeOps.  
**Score:** Good

## Section 5 — Template Assessment
Morning, Bedtime, and Homework routines are **long-term workflow tools** for me when I am still learning, but they should look very simple. After 90 days, I may know the routine and need the screen less.

**Reviewer Verdict:** Helpful until I remember the routine.  
**Score:** Good

## Section 6 — Stale Data Risk Assessment
### What Accumulates
- Old tasks.
- Old goals.
- Old happy memories.

### Highest-Risk Areas
1. Old tasks I do not understand.
2. Too many words.
3. Old goals that are not mine anymore.

### Lowest-Risk Areas
1. Today’s chore.
2. One current goal.
3. A few special memories.

**Reviewer Verdict:** Keep my screen tidy.  
**Score:** Good

## Section 7 — Maintenance Burden Assessment
I should not have much maintenance. I can mark things done. Grown-ups should clean old things.

### Biggest Maintenance Risks
- Too many tasks.
- Too much reading.
- Same recognition every time.

### Biggest Maintenance Wins
- I know what to do.
- I feel proud when I help.

**Parent burden:** Parents keep it tidy.  
**Child burden:** Low.

**Reviewer Verdict:** Good if my part is small.  
**Score:** Good

## Section 8 — Input Friction Assessment
- **Tasks:** I can complete them, not create many.
- **Shopping:** Maybe with help.
- **Goals:** A grown-up helps.
- **Helpful Moments:** I like seeing them, not writing them.

### Would Users Avoid Entering Data?
I would avoid typing. I would tap simple buttons.

**Reviewer Verdict:** Good if I mostly tap.  
**Score:** Good

## Section 9 — Trust Assessment
I trust it if it shows today’s real things. I stop trusting it if it shows old stuff or too many things.

### Trust Strengths
- Simple current tasks.
- One goal.
- Happy recognition.

### Trust Risks
- Old chores.
- Too many messages.
- Confusing lists.

**One-Year Trust Verdict:** I would probably trust it if parents keep it simple.  
**Score:** Good

## Section 10 — Biggest Remaining Problem
The biggest problem is **too much old stuff on my screen**.

**Existing area to improve next:** A weekly cleanup by the family so my tasks and goal stay easy.

## Final Independent Answer
HomeOps **mostly** saves effort because it helps me know what to do and feel proud, but I need it to stay simple.
