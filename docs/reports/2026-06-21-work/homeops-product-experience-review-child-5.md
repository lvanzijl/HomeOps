# HomeOps Product Experience Review — Round 4 — Child Persona Age 5

## Reviewer lens
Age 5 child. Wants recognition, fun, and simplicity. This review evaluates current product experience only.

## Section 1 — Home

### Strengths
- I can see my family people.
- I can tap my picture/name to go to my page.
- The celebration picture/party idea can be fun if someone explains it.

### Weaknesses
- Home has too many grown-up things.
- Agenda, lists, and tasks feel like parent stuff.
- I do not immediately know what I should care about.
- I need an adult to explain most of Home.

### Score
Needs Work

### Reviewer verdict
Home does not feel like my family hub yet. It feels like a grown-up family board.

## Section 2 — Child Workspace

### Mandatory questions

#### What does a child see first?
I see me: my picture, my name, and what I am working on.

#### What does a parent see first?
My parent sees my page and a Parent Mode button.

#### Does the page belong to the child or the parent?
This page mostly feels like mine, but some parts still sound like grown-ups talking.

### Strengths
- I like seeing my picture big.
- “This is me” is easy.
- I like seeing that my family noticed something good I did.
- A party or celebration sounds fun.
- I like simple progress when it looks big and visual.

### Weaknesses
- There are too many words.
- “What I am working on” and progress numbers can be hard.
- “A grown-up can add a goal” sounds like the page is not really mine.
- Tasks feel like being told what to do.
- Celebration needs to look more exciting if I am supposed to want it.

### Score
Good

### Reviewer verdict
My page feels more like a child page than a parent screen. I like seeing myself and thank-you notes. I still need more pictures, fun, and simple words.

## Section 3 — Motivation

### Strengths
- I can see stars and progress.
- I can understand “we are working together” if a parent explains it.
- Thank-you notes feel good.

### Weaknesses
- Motivation has lots of grown-up words.
- I do not want to edit goals.
- I care more about what fun thing happens when we finish.

### Score
Needs Work

### Reviewer verdict
Motivation mostly feels like grown-up planning with some fun parts.

## Section 4 — Helpful Moments

### Strengths
- I like when it says my family noticed me.
- “Thank you for” feels good.
- It makes me feel proud.

### Weaknesses
- I need someone to read the notes.
- It could be more like a special card or sticker.
- I want it to feel bigger when I did something kind.

### Score
Good

### Reviewer verdict
Helpful Moments feel like things my family appreciates.

## Section 5 — Celebration

### Mandatory question: Why would a child look forward to this?
I would look forward to it if it is something fun like movie night, popcorn, pancakes, a park trip, or a family game. I need to see it and feel excited.

### Strengths
- The party icon is fun.
- I like knowing we get something together.
- “Ready to celebrate” sounds exciting.

### Weaknesses
- It does not look big enough.
- I cannot imagine the celebration very well from the screen.
- “Mark celebrated” is for parents, not me.
- I want a more exciting countdown or picture.

### Score
Needs Work

### Reviewer verdict
Celebration could be fun, but right now it is not exciting enough for me to keep checking.

## Section 6 — Family Story

### Strengths
- I can understand that helping makes progress.
- I can understand that progress leads to a family celebration.
- I like remembering fun things we did.

### Weaknesses
- The story has too many parts.
- I need an adult to explain how goal, progress, celebration, and memory connect.
- I mostly care about “What did I do?” and “What fun thing happens?”

### Score
Needs Work

### Reviewer verdict
It feels like some family story, but not simple enough for me yet.

## Section 7 — Age Differentiation

### Strengths
- My page has some simpler parts.
- Big avatar and big progress are good for me.

### Weaknesses
- There is still too much reading.
- It does not feel like a page made only for little kids.
- I need more pictures, sounds, stickers, or simple choices.

### Score
Needs Work

### Reviewer verdict
The product knows I am little, but it still talks like it is for older kids and parents.

## Section 8 — Product Cohesion

### Strengths
- I can find my page from Home.
- My page, thank-you notes, and celebrations feel connected.

### Weaknesses
- Home, Tasks, and Lists feel like parent areas.
- Motivation is partly fun and partly grown-up.
- The product is not always for me.

### Score
Needs Work

### Reviewer verdict
Some parts feel like one family product, but many parts feel like grown-up tools.

## Section 9 — Biggest missing piece

### One major improvement
Make my page and celebration more fun and visual.

### Why
I want to see myself, see what I did, and see the fun thing coming.

### Product problem solved
I would understand why I should care without a grown-up explaining everything.

### Experience change
I would want to open my page because it feels happy, proud, and simple.
