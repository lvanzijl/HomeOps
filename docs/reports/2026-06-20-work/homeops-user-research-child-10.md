# Persona Simulation: Child Age 10

## Persona
Reads well, likes progress and achievements, wants ownership.

## Reading the Guide
A ten-year-old can understand Tasks, their personal goals, and helpful moments. They notice that rewards and achievements are not really available.

## Empty Installation Walkthrough

| Step | What they try | Success? | Struggle / understanding |
|---|---|---|---|
| Configure household | Parent-led setup | Partial | They can follow but should not own setup. |
| Add family members | Confirms their profile | Yes with parent | Understands roster. |
| Configure avatars | Edits own avatar | Yes | Engaging and gives ownership. |
| Configure goals | Adds or asks parent to add personal goal | Yes with help | Progress markers are understandable. |
| Configure rewards | Looks for rewards/achievements | No | Gamification placeholder and no reward redemption weaken motivation. |
| Configure tasks | Uses assigned tasks | Partial | Can complete/reopen tasks, but no dedicated child dashboard is proven. |
| Configure shopping lists | Adds grocery items if asked | Partial | Usable but not central to motivation. |
| Configure calendars | Reads agenda | Partial | Can view events; likely not configure recurrence/reminders. |
| Multiple hypothetical weeks | Tracks progress | Moderate | Personal goals and helpful moments provide ownership, but missing rewards/achievements reduce long-term excitement. |

## Assessment
For age 10, child engagement is **Moderate**. The child can use more of the product than the six-year-old, especially personal goals and tasks, but ownership is limited without achievements/rewards and a child-focused view.
