# Persona Simulation: Mother

## Persona
Intelligent, non-technical, wants simplicity, avoids administration.

## Reading the Guide
She likes that the guide is organized by visible navigation labels. She is concerned by features described as placeholders or limitations because she expects the product to guide her without reading documentation.

## Empty Installation Walkthrough

| Step | What she tries | Success? | Struggle / understanding |
|---|---|---|---|
| Configure household | Follows first-run wizard | Partial | Simple member setup succeeds, but she is not guided to tasks, lists, calendar, or goals afterward. |
| Add family members | Adds adults and children | Yes | Clear enough; review step helps. |
| Configure avatars | Clicks family members from Home | Yes | Understands avatar editing after discovery; may not know it is available until clicking a member. |
| Configure goals | Uses Motivation empty state | Yes | “Create your first family goal” is understandable. Personal goals are more administrative. |
| Configure rewards | Looks for child rewards | No | Gamification placeholder creates confusion because it is visible in nav but not usable. |
| Configure tasks | Adds tasks | Partial | Can add tasks, but no recurring chores means too much ongoing admin. |
| Configure shopping lists | Creates list/items | Partial | Basic grocery use is easy; no rename/delete may become confusing after mistakes. |
| Configure calendars | Adds manual events | Partial | Manual entry is understandable; lack of reminders and recurring events undermines usefulness. |
| Multiple hypothetical weeks | Uses Home summaries and lists | Partial | Daily view is useful, but she tires of maintaining repeated tasks/events and explaining missing rewards to children. |

## Assessment
She values Home, Lists, Tasks, and simple goals, but adoption depends on reducing administration. Missing recurrence/reminders/rewards are the biggest barriers.
